Squats

Squats are a fundamental lower body exercise that targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. Follow this guide to perform squats correctly:

How to Perform Squats

1. Set Up: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes slightly pointed out. Keep your chest up and shoulders back.

2. Lower Your Body: Bend your knees and hips to lower your body as if you are sitting back into a chair. Keep your back straight and knees tracking over your toes.

3. Return to Start: Push through your heels to return to the starting position, fully extending your legs. Exhale as you rise.

4. Repetition: Perform the exercise for the desired number of repetitions. Start with bodyweight squats before adding weights.

Benefits

Squats provide several benefits:

  • Strengthens Lower Body: Targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes, enhancing lower body strength.
  • Improves Flexibility: Helps improve flexibility in the hips, knees, and ankles.
  • Enhances Core Stability: Engages the core muscles to maintain balance and stability during the squat.
  • Boosts Athletic Performance: Improves performance in activities that involve running, jumping, and lifting.

Safety Tips

To ensure a safe squatting experience:

  • Maintain Proper Form: Keep your back straight and chest up. Avoid letting your knees collapse inward.
  • Start with Bodyweight: Master the technique with bodyweight squats before progressing to weighted squats.
  • Warm Up: Perform a proper warm-up to prepare your muscles and joints for the exercise.
  • Use Proper Footwear: Wear supportive shoes to provide stability and prevent injury.
  • Listen to Your Body: If you experience pain or discomfort, stop and consult a fitness professional.
