Push-ups

Push-ups are a classic bodyweight exercise that targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps. Follow this guide to perform push-ups effectively:

How to Perform Push-ups

1. Set Up: Place your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart on the floor, with your body in a straight line from head to heels. Your feet should be close together.

2. Lower Your Body: Bend your elbows to lower your chest towards the floor, keeping your body in a straight line. Avoid letting your hips sag or rise.

3. Push Up: Push through your palms to extend your arms and return to the starting position. Exhale as you push up.

4. Repetition: Perform the exercise for the desired number of repetitions. Modify the exercise by doing knee push-ups if necessary.

Benefits

Push-ups offer several benefits:

  • Builds Upper Body Strength: Targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps, improving upper body strength.
  • Engages Core Muscles: Helps strengthen the core by requiring stabilization throughout the movement.
  • Improves Muscular Endurance: Enhances endurance in the upper body and core muscles.
  • Convenient Exercise: Can be performed anywhere with no equipment needed.

Safety Tips

To ensure a safe push-up experience:

  • Maintain Proper Form: Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels and avoid letting your hips sag.
  • Use a Proper Surface: Perform push-ups on a flat, stable surface to avoid injury.
  • Modify as Needed: Start with knee push-ups if you are unable to perform standard push-ups with proper form.
  • Listen to Your Body: If you feel any pain or discomfort, stop the exercise and consult a fitness professional.
